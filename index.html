About

3D Biophotonics &
Ocular Imaging

I am a PhD Candidate in Biomedical Engineering in the Jenkins Lab at Case Western Reserve University, where I develop novel 3D optical imaging tools to study the ocular surface at cellular and molecular resolution. My work directly informs our understanding of corneal innervation, ocular surface disease, and meibomian gland biology.

My dissertation research centers on 3D multiplexed virtual histology — combining spinning disk confocal microscopy, IHC, and RNA-FISH to spatially map molecular expression in intact ocular tissues without traditional sectioning. I also develop deep learning and AI tools to classify nerve fiber types and quantify structural patterns from large-scale 3D datasets.

Prior to CWRU, I earned a BS in Mechanical Engineering and Cognitive Neuroscience from Washington University in St. Louis, where I conducted research in thermal fluids engineering and cognitive aging.

Research Focus Areas

🔬
3D Virtual Histology
Designing and optimizing protocols for multiplexed 3D imaging of intact ocular tissues — meibomian glands, cornea, and eyelid — using confocal microscopy and multicolor fluorescence labeling, without traditional sectioning
⚡
Functional Corneal Nerve Imaging
Building tools for fast volumetric calcium imaging of corneal nerves in vivo using spinning disk confocal microscopy to capture transient neural activity and assess corneal innervation patterns
🤖
AI & Deep Learning for Tissue Analysis
Engineering deep learning tools to classify nerve fiber types, segment tissue structures, and quantify spatial molecular patterns from large-scale 3D microscopy datasets
🧬
Spatial Molecular Mapping
Developing IHC + RNA-FISH protocols for high-resolution spatial mapping of molecular expression in ocular surface tissues to understand neural control and disease mechanisms
